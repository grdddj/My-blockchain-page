Sources of information

Not so far ago, it was almost impossible to come across quality information and details about cryptocurrencies and blockchain. However, especially in the last two years, a lot of content started to be created in this space, so it is possible to gain a lot of knowledge on one place, without the need to browse through forums with sometimes questionable information.

  • Andreas Antonopoulos

    A. Antonopoulos is one of the biggest advocates and popularisators of blockchain and cryptocurrencies. He is an author of multiple books, ranging from philosophical stories about the importance cryptocurrencies to highly technical introductions of inner workings of Bitcoin and Ethereum. Majority of these books are available as open source, and Andreas is also running a youtube channel, where he posts free content almost every day. I personally get the most information about blockchain and crypto space from him.

  • Ivan on Tech

    Ivan on Tech is a young and very active youtuber, that is covering a lot of topics in cryptospace, from economics to technical aspects. He is also author of a variety of high-quality courses about the blockchain and its programming.

  • Dapp University

    Gregory from Dapp University is a relatively new educator in this space, and has already achieved quite a lot. He is concentrating especially on building decentralised applications (Dapps) on Ethereum blockchain. Apart from youtube videos he is also having a website with a lot of nice tutorials, which are very helpful and easy to follow.

    Today, there are already numerous quality sources of information about cryptocurrencies and blockchain, in the form of various web pages like Medium or Hackernoon with lots of crypto-articles, or other here not-mentioned youtube channels.
